High-level interests

I want to be right more often. I want to be wrong less often.

To accomplish the above goals using data, I study statistics in order to faithfully and accurately shape my decisions and opinions. I am interested in all manner of applications, from business problems to the social, life, and physical sciences.

Computers are an invaluable tool for accomplishing these tasks. Therefore, I also chose to study computer science, and I have come to enjoy constructing clean, readable code to solve problems.

For everything that I do, I seek to explain and communicate it clearly.

Specific areas of interest

  • Causal inference gives us tools to answer the myriad interesting questions of the form, "did A cause B, and by how much"?
  • Hypothesis testing gives a framework for drawing conclusions from data. I like to think carefully about which conclusions I can and should draw from a data analysis, and hypothesis testing frameworks guide people in doing so.
  • Information theory gives tools and a language for describing uncertainty.
  • Unsupervised learning leverages computation to uncover hidden structure in data, allowing people to learn things that otherwise would have been difficult to observe.
  • Computational reproducibility is more or less a necessary condition of good science or analysis. At minimum, your future self will thank you for caring about it.
